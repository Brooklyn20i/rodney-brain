## Summary

UPenn and BU researchers have mathematically modelled what happens when widespread AI-driven automation collapses consumer demand — the "AI Layoff Trap". Companies are caught in a Prisoner's Dilemma: automate and survive short-term, but if everyone automates simultaneously, mass unemployment kills revenue. Standard policy fixes (UBI, profit taxes) are proven insufficient; the researchers' proposed solution is a Pigouvian "robot tax" on automation.

## Why It Matters

This is the macro-economic constraint sitting underneath every enterprise AI transformation argument. The individual operator case for AI is clear — but the systemic demand-side risk is real and now formalised in peer-reviewed research. For any leader thinking about AI at scale, this is the policy and economic context that will eventually force regulatory intervention.
