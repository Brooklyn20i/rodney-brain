# AI is everywhere. But most companies are still stuck in pilot mode
**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Source:** https://x.com/mckinsey/status/2044808446424297635?s=51
**Type:** X Post
**Stance:** Aligned
**Tags:** #AI #Transformation #OperatingModel #Workflow #Leadership

## Summary
McKinsey argues that many organizations are stuck in "pilot mode" with AI because they’re treating AI as a technology add-on rather than redesigning the work. The constraint is not model capability but unchanged workflows, roles, and decision rights. They suggest leaders are beginning to rethink end-to-end workflows and how decisions get made. Real value comes when the operating model evolves alongside the technology.

## Why It Matters
Most AI programs plateau when they optimize isolated use cases instead of re-architecting how value is created and decisions flow. For senior leaders, the work is less about model selection and more about redesigning processes, redefining roles, and re-setting governance so AI can be embedded safely at scale. The competitive gap will widen between organizations that industrialize AI into core workflows and those that remain in perpetual experimentation.

## Key Quotes / Data Points
