**Author:** The Smart Ape 🔥 (@the_smart_ape) | **Date:** 12 April 2026 | **Source:** https://x.com/the_smart_ape/status/2043262727922053128

i built a research system for 4 clients in the past 6 months. one media company (well-known, can't name them), two consulting firms, and one independent analyst.

combined, they cut their research costs by about 60%. one of them fired 3 junior researchers and replaced them with this system + one senior editor who reviews the output.

no fancy tools. no $500/mo subscriptions. no 47 open chrome tabs.

just a folder of .md files, one ai agent (claude), and a system that takes one research question and produces a multi-angle analysis no single Google search will ever give you.

### what is a research skill graph?

most people use ai for research like this: open chatgpt, type [question]. that's not research. that's just a google search with extra steps.

a research skill graph is a folder of interconnected markdown files where each file is one node/skill/lens. when you point claude at this folder and give it a research question, it researches through 6 different lenses simultaneously.

the difference: a single prompt gives you a summary. a skill graph gives you a research department.

### why 6 lenses instead of one big prompt?

- **technical:** what do the numbers actually say? look at data only
- **economic:** follow the money. who pays, who profits, what incentives drive behavior
- **historical:** what patterns repeat? what's been tried before? what context is everyone forgetting
- **geopolitical:** zoom out to the global chessboard. which countries, which power dynamics
- **contrarian:** what if the consensus is wrong? who benefits from the current narrative
- **first principles:** forget everything. rebuild from fundamental truths only

Each lens produces findings that often contradict the others. That tension between lenses is where the real insight lives.

### the 20-file folder structure

- index.md (command center / operating system)
- research-frameworks.md (how to approach different question types)
- source-evaluation.md (5-tier trust system from primary to noise)
- synthesis-rules.md (how to combine findings without creating false consensus)
- contradiction-protocol.md (what to do when sources disagree — this is where the real insights hide)
- lenses/technical.md, economic.md, historical.md, geopolitical.md, contrarian.md, first-principles.md
- outputs/executive-summary.md, deep-dive.md, key-players.md, open-questions.md
- knowledge/concepts.md, data-points.md (accumulate across all projects)
- research-log.md (tracks every project with key findings and connections)
- source-template.md (copy for each major source processed)

### the compound effect

knowledge/concepts.md and knowledge/data-points.md accumulate across ALL research projects. After 10 projects, you have a proprietary knowledge base no AI tool can replicate.

open-questions from one research become the index.md of the next. Compound interest, but for knowledge.

### how to use it

**Method 1: Claude Projects** — upload all files, give it a topic. Runs the full system.
**Method 2: Paste context** — copy index.md + relevant lens files into any AI chat.
**Method 3: Claude Code + Obsidian** — point Claude Code at your local vault. Agent reads, writes, and updates markdown files autonomously. You watch the graph build in real time. Most powerful.

### why this beats traditional research

Traditional research: open 50 tabs, read 20 articles that all say the same thing, miss the contrarian view.

Skill graph research: one question through 6 forced angles, each with evaluation criteria, contradictions surfaced, synthesis structured. The contrarian lens is the biggest differentiator — traditional research almost never challenges its own assumptions.

**Build it in 1 hour:** create folder structure (5 min), fill index.md, fill 6 lens files, fill methodology files, upload to Claude project, test with a topic, iterate.
