## Summary
Brian Armstrong argues that "agentic commerce" (software agents transacting with other agents) is not yet reflected in market expectations. He suggests machine-to-machine payments will increase demand for the digital dollar beyond current forecasts. He further claims the resulting agentic economy could outgrow the human economy. He positions Coinbase as infrastructure for both.

## Why It Matters
If machines become first-class economic actors, the volume and speed of transactions could jump by orders of magnitude, forcing enterprises to rethink identity, authorization, payments rails, and risk controls as product primitives. Strategy shifts from digitizing existing workflows to designing for autonomous systems that buy, sell, and negotiate on behalf of customers and internal functions. Leaders should anticipate new platform battles around trust, settlement, and compliance for machine-native commerce.
